Cost of living

This varies, according to where in the UK you decide to study. London and other big cities are more expensive than other areas. There is no precise calculator which could tell you how much you need to live in the UK. If a website or any other source claims that- "This is the amount which you need for a month in the UK", they are wrong. The reason is- No body can calcluate the amount of your lifestyle.

For example- If you have been told that you need £1000 to live for a month in this country and you spent £1000 on one thing, they are getting proven to ben wrong.

So, We have added a rough guide with which you can calclulate what it could cost you for the major items you will need when you are in the UK:

Food

If you buy your own food, try and budget in about £25 to £30 per week to purchase food supplies. You can of course be smart and savvy about this and save much more.

Telephone(Landline)

The cost of mobiles varies depending on the provider and tariff you are on. Prepaid is generally cheaper to control costs, but if you make a lot of calls a contract will start at £5/ week. There are number of ways to call internationally and £5/week would be more than enough for you.

Internet

This depends on what sort of plan you need. There are two options- Standard DSL and Fiber. With standard DSL broadband, you will be paying around £20/month and fibre may cost you an extra £10 - £15 / month.

Bills

If you live in a house or apartment you will need to calculate water, heating and electricity into your budget - this can be anything from £10/week for all bills depending on the time of year.

Study

Materials Books, notepads, paper, printer cartridges are all things you will need and you should budget about £7/ week for this.

Fun

It’s not all study when you come to the UK, there will be time for some fun so you need to think about the cost of this. A cinema ticket in the UK costs around £8, a pint of beer £3. These are all costs you will need to budget so set aside what you’ll need for fun!
